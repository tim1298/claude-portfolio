Context

The opportunity, and the catch

This project was created at a large-scale AI MarTech company serving enterprise marketing teams.

Marketers want to target audiences based on nuanced behaviors, preferences, and intent. But analyzing individual attributes across millions of site visitors is nearly impossible to do manually.

AI could help, but existing tools often created a different problem: marketers did not know whether they could trust the output.

  • Opaque — AI predictions were hard to explain
  • Unverifiable — no clear way to test, correct, or override results
  • Risky — costly to apply at scale without confidence in accuracy
Problem

A "look what we can do" prototype, not yet a product

Before I joined, the team had built a simulation tool that generated real-time attribute predictions, such as demographics, hobbies, and brand preferences, based on a tester's own browsing behavior.

The technology was impressive, but the use case was limited.

  • Insights came from biased simulations, not real customer behavior
  • There was no practical workflow beyond demonstrating technical capability
  • The output was interesting, but not something marketers could confidently act on

The product needed to shift from a technical showcase to a real marketing intelligence tool.

Goal

Create an intuitive AI workflow that helps marketers define user attributes, review and correct LLM-generated predictions, understand how predictions are made, and confidently apply those insights across a large audience.

The goal was not just faster segmentation. It was building long-term trust in AI-powered decision-making.

How it works

A human-in-the-loop prediction workflow

01

Define the attribute

Marketers start by defining what they want to learn about their customers, such as a user's preferred TV display technology.

02

Review sample predictions

They review a curated set of real user profiles with predicted values, confidence scores, and plain-language explanations. This helps them test accuracy, give feedback, and refine the model before applying it at scale.

03

View distribution and apply

Once the prediction logic is refined, the system projects how the attribute is distributed across the full user base. Marketers can then assess segment potential and apply the attribute to targeted campaigns, such as promoting OLED TVs to users most likely to prefer them.

Approach

Redefine the problem before designing the screens

Redefine the problem

I challenged the assumption that "simulation" was the core experience. Through workshops and cross-functional discussions, I helped redirect the team toward generating user attributes from real behavioral data.

Create strategic value

That reframing turned a stagnant sandbox feature into a scalable marketing intelligence workflow, from attribute definition to prediction refinement to audience segmentation.

Align and mentor the team

I bridged PM, engineering, AI research, and UX around a shared product direction. I also mentored junior designers through information architecture, interaction patterns, and stakeholder communication.

UX challenges

Designing for trust in AI

Challenge 01

How do we make the AI workflow intuitive?

I streamlined the attribute creation process, automated value generation, supported CSV uploads, and combined setup with real-time prediction examples so the workflow became easier to learn as users moved through it.

Challenge 02

How do we help marketers trust AI predictions?

I surfaced confidence scores, explanations, and live preview examples so marketers could understand how predictions were generated before applying them to a wider audience.

Challenge 03

How do we encourage feedback without making the workflow feel demanding?

I reduced information overload by showing one user card at a time and made feedback actions clear and lightweight, helping users improve prediction accuracy without slowing them down.

Validated with clients

Assumptions we tested

  • What helps marketers trust AI predictions at first glance?
  • Which data points are most useful for verifying prediction accuracy?
  • Are random or filtered demographic previews more useful for evaluation?
Results
  • Established trust in AI tagging workflows
  • Reduced time-to-segmentation from days to minutes
  • Introduced a flexible system that is scalable and auditable
  • Helped marketers apply AI-generated insights with more confidence and control
Takeaway

Building trust in AI takes more than accurate predictions. It requires transparency, control, and a clear way for humans to verify what the model is doing.

Keeping marketers in the loop did not just improve accuracy. It made the system easier to trust, adopt, and scale.
